Abstract

We present ConicLoc, a map-free monocular visual localization method that leverages time-invariant conic geometric primitives in structured environments for estimating the six degrees of freedom (DoF) camera poses. Existing visual localization approaches depend on handcrafted keypoints, semantic cues, or pre-built maps, making them vulnerable to illumination changes, occlusions, and frequent environmental reconfigurations. To overcome these limitations, we exploit time-invariant conic geometry and Manhattan world (MW) regularities that naturally arise in human-made environments. We first propose a Perspective-Two-Circle (P2C) that leverages the known radii of two circular structures to recover an initial camera pose without requiring depth measurements or prior maps. Given the conic normals from P2C, we infer dominant Manhattan directions and achieve drift-free orientation estimation by integrating MW-aligned lines. We further refine 6-DoF camera poses through a novel conic bundle adjustment (CBA) with algebraic conic residuals. We evaluate the proposed method on synthetic and real-world datasets, including free-flying robot sequences collected on the International Space Station (ISS). Despite operating in a fully map-free monocular setting, the localization accuracy of the proposed ConicLoc is comparable to existing map-based visual localization methods, demonstrating that ConicLoc enables reliable global pose estimation even in highly complex and challenging environments without relying on pre-built maps.
